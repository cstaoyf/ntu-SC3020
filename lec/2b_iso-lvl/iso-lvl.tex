\begin{frame}
\begin{small}
    This lecture focuses on \bred{concurrent schedules}. We will see that they can cause different types of ``strange'' phenomena --- called \blue{anomalies} --- that can never arise in serial schedules. A database system may choose to allow \bred{none or some} of those anomalies. Such a policy is formalized by specifying an appropriate \bred{isolation level}.

\end{small}
